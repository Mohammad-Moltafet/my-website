Mohammad Moltafet - About me

I received my Ph.D. in communications engineering from the University of Oulu, Finland, in 2021. I was a Visiting Ph.D. Researcher at Linköping University, Sweden, in 2019, and a Postdoctoral Researcher at the University of Oulu, and the Technical University of Munich (TUM), Germany, in 2021 and 2022, respectively. Currently, I am a Research Associate at the University of California at Santa Cruz, USA.

Research Interests and Background

My current research interests include:

  • Semantic communications with special emphasis on information freshness, goal-oriented communications, and real-time remote tracking
  • Cybersecurity with special emphasis on information-theoretic security, multi-party communications in IoT systems, data storage, and federated learning
  • Reinforcement learning and decision-making under uncertainty, including Markov Decision Processes (MDPs), Constrained MDPs (CMDPs), and Partially Observable MDPs (POMDPs)
  • Next-generation wireless systems and networking, including radio access networks, multiple access techniques, optimization, and stochastic control for both broadband data networks, i.e., 5/6G, and wireless sensor networks
  • Optimization techniques for wireless communications and signal processing, including convex optimization, Lyapunov optimization, machine learning, distributed optimization, large-scale optimization, and dynamic programming

The core theoretical background required for carrying out successful research in these fast-developing fields is formed by: Communication Theory and Networking, Mathematical Optimization, Information Theory, Machine Learning, Probability Theory, Queuing Theory, Signal Processing, and Number Theory.
